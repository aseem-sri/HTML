Debugging the Phantom Loop

I spent three agonizing days tracking down a memory leak that kept crashing my game's frame rate during intense combat scenes. The culprit turned out to be a single, misplaced line of code that kept spawning invisible enemies.

Deleting that one erroneous string brought the performance back to a buttery-smooth sixty frames per second, bringing instant relief.

Designing Characters with Only 16 Pixels

Creating an expressive video game hero within a strict sixteen-by-sixteen pixel grid is a fascinating artistic challenge. Every single square of color must serve a distinct purpose to convey movement, personality, and emotion.

By limiting my color palette to just five shades, I forced myself to focus purely on silhouette and fluid animation cycles.

My First Indie Game Launch

After six months of sleepless nights and endless code compilation, my puzzle platformer is finally live on the global marketplace. Watching players from around the world stream my game and solve the levels is a surreal experience.

The feedback has been incredibly motivating, and I am already sketching out ideas for the upcoming expansion pack.
